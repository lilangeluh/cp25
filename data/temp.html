A Method for Remembering Weather

This project began with a simple question: what if temperature wasn't just data, but autobiography? What if the body kept its own climate record?

Each of these 40 memories corresponds to a specific temperature between 0°F and 100°F. They span four cities—Shenzhen, San Diego, Los Angeles, and Providence—and trace the arc of migration, education, and becoming.

The temperatures are real, pulled from official weather records (NOAA, JMA, local stations). The memories are real too, though compressed and distilled. Together, they form a kind of dual record: meteorological and emotional, quantified and felt.

Climate is often discussed in abstractions—degrees of warming, tipping points, futures. But we experience it in our bodies first. In the numbness of fingers. In sweat on the back of the neck. In the specific quality of light at 67°F on a spring evening in Los Angeles.

This is an attempt to honor that embodied knowledge. To say: I was here. I felt this. The body remembers.

Data sources: NOAA, Japan Meteorological Agency, local weather stations

Memory period: 2003–2025

Locations: Shenzhen, China · San Diego, CA · Los Angeles, CA · Providence, RI
