About

Temperatures My Body Remembers is a dataset built from forty-five memories—each tied to its real-world weather, location, and atmosphere. It treats climate not as background noise but as a kind of embodied metadata: the part of a moment your body registers first and forgets last.

Each entry blends climate data with lived detail: a narrative, a small map, coordinates, a body index, and an image. Arranged from coldest to warmest, the collection becomes a thermal timeline—another way to read some of my fondest memories.

I wanted to ask how data can hold emotional truth, and how air, heat, cold, and humidity shape what we remember. It’s part archive, part diary, part quietly dramatic weather report :DD
